WELCOME!

This is the official site of Vikings Dev, the coding club of Burnaby North Secondary. Our aim is to promote computer science within an educational environment and assist today's high schoolers in becoming tomorrow's innovators and industry leaders!

WHO WE ARE

Vikings Dev is an after-school club that operates every in-session Wednesday at Burnaby North Secondary. Meetings run from 3:03 PM to 4:30 PM and new members are always welcome. For more information, join our Discord server or check out our social media!

RESOURCES

At Vikings Dev, we run a number of project-based courses dealing with programming related subjects such as game design, web design, and Discord bots. Along with that, we host occasional lessons in various topics including blockchain, neural networks, machine learning, and object oriented programming. In our student-developed courses, we encourage coders to pursue club-directed independent projects related to the topics being taught. To learn more, click the link below.

FAQ

Q: Who is allowed to participate?
A: Anyone who is currently a high school student at Burnaby North Secondary is allowed to participate in club activities. Courses will be open sourced so anyone can access the resources we are using for the club. Some courses will be uploading lessons, examples and all courses will be uploading starter kits, that are free for everyone to use!

Q: When will I be able to join?
A: Our first meeting will be on October 3, 2018 from 3:10 PM to 4:30 PM in room 315. Vikings Dev will officially begin meetings after Clubs Day, but new members are welcome anytime.

Q: Do I need to know how to code to join?
A: Prior experience is not required, as Vikings Dev is open to coders of all levels.

Q: What if I'm already an experienced programmer?
A: Skilled coders in particular are encouraged to come, since the goal of our club is not only to create a welcoming environment for beginners, but to cultivate a diverse community of coders to collaborate and network with. We highly encourage talented students to come and help, by becoming a Project Leader or mentor.

Q: I am a parent/teacher/sponsor. Who should I contact?
A: You can contact us at our official email address: vikingsdev@gmail.com
