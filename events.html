Upcoming Hackathons

Here's a curated list of hackathons that are eligible for high school students in Vancouver!



xdHacks Mini 2019

 TBA - Early 2019 UBC - Hennings Building

High-school students are the innovators of tomorrow and, in Vancouver especially, there are not many events which inspire students to create a product and present it. Hackathons, in particular, enable students to apply their interests, passions, and skills to create a product to help solve society’s current issues through technology. XdHacks, being Vancouver’s first high-school Hackathon provides a new type of opportunity for tomorrows aspiring tech professionals.



nwHacks 2020

 TBA - Early 2019 UBC

Come make things and break things, and then make them cooler. You'll never be short on inspiration when you’re surrounded by 650 of the brightest minds in the Pacific Northwest. All you need to bring is an open mind and an insatiable desire to learn; we’ll take care of the rest. After all, we're western Canada’s largest hackathon — we make the west coast the best coast.



cmd-f

 TBA - Early 2019 UBC

An all-female 24 hour hackathon, hosted at the University of British Columbia, where students will come together on a journey to innovate, inspire, and learn. We welcome all participants who identify as female, non-binary, agender, or intersex. You do not have to be a UBC student to attend; high school students, post-secondary students, and recent graduates (graduation date within one year of January 2019) are all eligible to participate.



vhHacks 2020

 March 14-15, 2020 UBC

vhHacks is Greater Vancouver's largest hackathon dedicated to high school students (like you!) More than one hundred students will gather and collaborate to build and showcase a project in under 24 hours. No experience? No problem! vhHacks is beginner friendly, so anyone can join and learn a new skill!



lumohacks

 Date TBA Location TBA

Lumohacks is a 24-hour hackathon where we will use tech to improve the lives of people facing mental health challenges, especially from the military and first responder communities. Lumohacks is open to everyone who share the passion for healthcare and tech!
